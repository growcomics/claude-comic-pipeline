## Growth-page accounting
- **Growth-page ratio (rubric definition: growth_state ∈ {trigger,early,mid,peak} OR role==active-growth): 4 / 19 = 21%.** Qualifying pages: **p9** (early), **p10** (mid / active-growth), **p13** (peak / active-growth), **p14** (peak / active-growth). The two prologue hero-splashes (p3 devil, p4 angel) are `action`, not growth — they reveal already-grown forms.
- Secondary "felt growth product" coverage (pages that *display* the new bodies but score `aftermath`, so they fall OUTSIDE the ratio above): add **p11** (buff hedonistic aftermath) and **p12** (full-body new-body reveal) → ~6/19 ≈ 32% of pages are visibly about big muscle in some form. Reported headline ratio remains the strict **4/19 = 21%**.
- Transformation scenes:
  - **Gym strength-feat & muscle pump** — p9–10. Devices: multi-panel-progressive, zoom-escalation, size-comparison (1000KG plates / "MAX LEVEL 4000KG" sign), sfx-driven (BAAM!!→SLAM!!×3→CRAAANK!), reaction-intercut (gym crowd).
  - **Angel & devil parallel transformation** — p13–14. Devices: multi-panel-progressive, clothing-destruction (p13.3 KRAAK!/BREAK chest-burst), zoom-escalation (CU→ECU), size-comparison (p14 skyline + crushed car), reaction-intercut (p13 face/face cross-cut), sfx-driven, full-body-reveal (p14 city-scale).
- Zoom coverage: strong but concentrated. Page 13 is essentially all CU/ECU body-part money-shots (face, chest, abs, legs); page 10 closes on an MCU strain. Outside those two pages, growth-focus panels are mostly WS/MS — the book front-loads camera variety but rations true ECU growth detail to the climactic transformation.

## Camera
- **Distance spread:** wide across the book. Multiple full-page splashes use EWS (p2) and low-hero WS (p3, p4); the climax reaches city-scale EWS (p14.2, p17.3). Per-page spreads are healthy — p5 hits four distinct bands (WS/MLS/WS/CU), p9/p10/p12/p17 each span three. The book almost never repeats one distance down a page.
- **Flat-panel hotspots:** the single real offender is **p8** — a six-panel angel-vs-devil debate where 5/6 panels are `flat-level` talking heads. Minor flat crops also appear in the p6 face-strip row (p6.1–6.3, decorative ECU/MCU). Everything else stays dynamic.
- **Best dynamic compositions to steal:**
  - **p3 / p4** — mirrored full-page low-hero hero-splashes (devil descending / angel descending into a ring of smaller figures): clean scale-contrast + foreshortening diptych.
  - **p7.2** — a dutch-angle, high-vantage whip-brawl (THWACK!) with bodies flung on diagonals; best single action staging.
  - **p13** — the 6-panel parallel-transformation grid (see strengths).
  - **p14.3** — kaiju foot crushing a street (THOOM×3) with a flung car: textbook size-comparison growth payoff.
  - **p17.3** — page-wide EWS energy detonation over the skyline.

## Expressions
- Faces carry the **peaks** exactly where the rubric wants them: **p13.1/p13.2** ecstasy at 5/5 (devil & angel mid-transformation howls), **p10.3** strain at 5/5 (CRAAANK pump-yell). The hero/villain splashes (p3 rage, p4 smug) also land at 4.
- **Dead-face / underplayed defects:** the **p6** opening face-crop strips sit at intensity 2 (decorative, emotionless) in a context that should intrigue; **p14.2** (the wide aerial reveal) and **p15.2** under-register given the scale of the moment. These are the spots a clearer reaction would have sold the beat.
- No outright peak-growth-with-neutral-face defect: the genuinely climactic growth panels (p13) all carry strong faces or are intentional body-part ECUs with no face in frame (p13.4–6), which is acceptable framing rather than a dead face.

## Story & structure
- **Hook:** fast and loud — opens in-media-res on a full-realm EWS (p2) then back-to-back hero splashes (p3 devil, p4 angel). The page-1 promise (cover face-off over a giant glaring face) is "two muscular goddesses, angel vs devil, will clash." It grabs visually immediately.
- **Pacing:** front-loaded action, then a talky lull (p6–8), then two separate growth movements (gym p9–10, angel/devil p13–14) bridged by a hedonistic aftermath (p11) that stalls momentum. The true progressive-growth payoff (p13) arrives very late for a 19-page book.
- **Tease vs payoff (relevant to the redhead-houseguest edging structure):** this is a **delayed-release** book, not an edging one — it teases the transformed forms in the prologue (p3–5), withholds for the setup/gym middle, then **delivers fully** at p13–14 (the actual on-panel transformation + city-scale reveal). It does NOT over-tease-and-deny; if anything it pays off too tersely (the money transformation is two pages). Lesson for the houseguest project: the prologue-flash-forward is a clean way to promise the payoff up front while still delaying the on-panel growth — but the delivery needs more than two pages of real estate or it feels rushed.
- **Cliffhanger / continuity:** weak. It ends mid-brawl on a FWOOOSSSH!!! charge (p18.4) — a momentum "to be continued" rather than a discrete reveal or twist. The shoulder-angel/devil conceit (p6) is the cleverest continuity thread but its argument never reads because the balloons are empty.
- **Dialogue & SFX:** lettering is **half-finished** — SFX are fully rendered and excellent (BAAM/SLAM/CRAAANK, KRAAK, the page-wide KRRIKKK-THOOM banner), but **every speech balloon in the book is an empty white placeholder** (p5, p8, p15, p18). The story is carried entirely by image + SFX; the verbal layer is missing.
- **Clarity:** panel-to-panel action reads cleanly; the conceptual layer (who the angel/devil are, why the protagonist transforms, how her gym arc connects to their transformation) is muddy precisely because the dialogue is absent.

**Narrative arc:** in-media-res splash of angel vs devil super-brawlers → flashback to a silver-haired woman with a tiny angel & devil on her shoulders (her conscience) → they argue/tempt her through a gym strength-growth montage and a hedonistic aftermath → the angel & devil themselves transform into giant muscle goddesses → they grow city-sized and clash → mid-fight cliffhanger (to be continued).

## Strengths to steal
- **The page-13 parallel-transformation grid** — a 6-panel page intercutting two characters growing at once: face→face (ecstasy 5/5), chest→chest (with a KRAAK!/BREAK clothing-burst), abs→legs ECUs. It stacks multi-panel-progressive + reaction-intercut + clothing-destruction + sfx-driven + ECU in a single page. This is the gold-standard device the pipeline wants more of.
- **SFX-as-sensation discipline** — escalating onomatopoeia carries growth/impact with zero dialogue: BAAM!! → SLAM!!×3 → CRAAANK! across the p10 pump, and the page-wide "KRRIKKK THOOOOO…OMMMM!!!" detonation banner on p17. Worth replicating: ramp the SFX size/repetition with the growth stage.
- **Mirrored hero-splash diptych (p3/p4)** — staging the two leads as matching full-page low-hero descents into rings of smaller figures is an efficient way to introduce rivals and bake in scale-contrast/foreshortening.
- **Kaiju size-comparison payoff (p14)** — putting the finished growth against a full skyline + crushed car + THOOM footsteps makes the magnitude legible instantly; a reusable "show the result against a fixed-scale reference" move.

## Weaknesses to avoid
- **Don't ration the growth.** Only 4/19 pages (21%) are active growth — even counting the show-off aftermath it's ~6/19 — and the true progressive transformation is just two pages (p13–14) at the very end. For an FMG product this under-serves the niche; budget more pages to on-panel inflation, earlier.
- **Don't split the transformation focus without connecting it.** The protagonist's gym/aftermath arc (p9–12) and the angel/devil transformation (p13–14) read as two separate growth events; the link is never spelled out (worsened by the empty dialogue).
- **Never ship empty balloons.** Every speech bubble in the book (p5, p8, p15, p18) is a blank placeholder; the verbal layer is missing, so character motivation and the angel/devil argument don't read. (Directly relevant to the pipeline's bake-dialogue-into-every-panel rule.)
- **Avoid the p8 flat-level talking-head row** — 5/6 panels are level, same-plane mid/close shots of an argument with no camera movement; it's the one genuinely flat stretch and it sits right where the story needs to convey motivation.
- **Don't end on pure momentum** — stopping mid-swing on a FWOOOSSSH charge (p18) is a soft "to be continued," not a hook; pair the last beat with a turn or reveal.
