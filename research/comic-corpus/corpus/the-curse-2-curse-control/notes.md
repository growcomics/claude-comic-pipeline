## Scores (0–5)

## Growth-page accounting
- Growth pages (growth_state ∈ trigger/early/mid/peak, or role==active-growth): pages 1,2,3,8,9,10,11,14,15,16,17,18,19,20,21 = **15 of 22** → **growth-page ratio = 68%** (p14 counts as the forced *trigger* for transformation #3). This sits well above the niche's "good" bar (~25–35%). Non-growth pages: 4,5,6,12,13 (fights/clash), 7 (domestic setup), 22 (credits).
- Transformation scenes (4 growth scenes + 2 fights):
  - **Transformation #1 — protagonist drinks (p1-3):** multi-panel-progressive, zoom-escalation, sfx-driven (KZZZT), full-body-reveal (p3), light clothing-destruction (sash torn).
  - **Transformation #2 — blonde at home (p7-11):** slow-burn (a full domestic page of build before the SHIIING trigger), multi-panel-progressive, zoom-escalation, sfx-driven, full-body-reveal (p10), size-comparison (p11 vs the man).
  - **Transformation #3 — protagonist force-fed (p15-17):** multi-panel-progressive, zoom-escalation, clothing-destruction (p17 fabric tears), sfx-driven, full-body-reveal (p16).
  - **Escalating arms-race (p18-21):** the two trade potion-fueled surges — clearest armor/clothing-destruction in the book (p18.2 gold band cracks off), size-comparison splash (p19 EWS), multi-panel-progressive, sfx-driven, with the genie-lamp source surfaced (p21).
  - Fights (p4-6, p12-14) are action, not growth, but reuse size-comparison + reaction-intercut + heavy SFX.
- Zoom coverage: very high. Each transformation runs a 4-panel ECU/CU grid hitting arms→abs/chest→glutes→legs (p2, p9, p15, p18), plus chest/torso ECUs on p8.5 and p17. This is the gold-standard progressive zoom the pipeline wants more of — though the four framings recycle nearly verbatim each surge.

## Camera
- **Distance spread across the book:** strong. Page-level spreads frequently hit 3–4 distinct bands (p2, p8, p9, p11, p12, p15, p17, p20, p21), ranging EWS (p19) through ECU (the growth grids). The four splash pages (p3, p10, p16, p19) punctuate the rhythm with WS/EWS reveals.
- **Flat-panel hotspots:** the domestic reset **page 7** is the one genuine failure — four flat-level eye-level mid-shots in a row (p7.2-7.5). Minor softness also at p13.3-13.4 (two near-identical gloat MCUs) and p1.2 (head-on reaction). Everything else stays dynamic.
- **Best dynamic compositions to steal:**
  - **p11** — towering white/gold heroine in FG with the tiny kneeling man in BG: textbook FG/BG scale-contrast + depth, distance spread MLS→ECU across four panels.
  - **p19** — EWS colossal figure with the human-scale man at her feet for size-comparison; magnitude sold by composition, not muscle size alone.
  - **p4 / p12** — fist-collisions on hard diagonals with foreshortened arms, capped by a dutch tilt (p4.3, p12.5) and a reaction-intercut CU (p12.4).
  - **p6.2** — tight grinning face streaking through a canted purple speed-band; cheap, effective motion device.
  - **p3 / p16** — low-hero airborne flight splashes that make the transformed figure tower.

## Expressions
- Faces generally **carry the beat**. Growth peaks reliably hit 4–5: ecstasy/strain at p2.2, p8.4, p9.1, p15.4, p17.1; rage/effort through the fights (p4.1, p5.2, p5.4); and a genuine fear/panic run on the force-feed (p14.2 grimace, p14.3 panic, p14.4 shock) that sells dread better than any line of (absent) dialogue.
- **Dead-face / weak spots:** the domestic page 7 underplays (joy at intensity 2–3 on near-blank faces, p7.2-7.5); the p13 gloat MCUs are merely "fine" (3). By design, the body-part ECUs in every growth grid are faceless — fair for the device, but it means the *peak* of several surges leans on the body, not the face, so the single hero-panel face per page (e.g. p15.4) has to do the emotional lifting.
- No peak-growth panel is outright neutral-when-it-shouldn't-be; the comic avoids the classic dead-face-at-climax defect.

## Story & structure
- **Hook:** instant but disorienting. P1 opens *in medias res* — the dark-haired woman already drinking the curse potion while the blonde recoils. No page-1 promise beyond "transformation is imminent"; great for immediacy, poor for legibility (this is issue #2 and assumes you read #1).
- **Pacing:** growth-forward to a fault. Setup is one page (p7) and one cold opening beat; the rest is transformation + fight + transformation + arms-race. It never drags on dialogue, but it also never *breathes* or builds character — it's a stim loop of surges.
- **Tease vs payoff:** mixed. The force-feed (p14) is the one real tease-then-release — she resists, it's forced, then it pays off into transformation #3 (p15-16). Otherwise the structure is closer to "dump and escalate": each potion delivers immediately, then another potion appears (p13, p17, p21), so anticipation is shallow. For the redhead-houseguest edging model, the lesson is mostly **negative** — this is the opposite of slow-burn delay; the one beat that works (p14 forced delay) is worth emulating, the rapid potion-refill cadence is what to avoid if you want sustained tease.
- **Cliffhanger / continuity:** weak. P21 ends on a smug blonde mid-arms-race with the genie-lamp as the only forward hook — no climax, no reversal, no clear "to be continued" beat beyond "they're still growing." It stops rather than lands.
- **Dialogue & SFX:** SFX are the strength (see strengths); dialogue is the hole — **many balloons are drawn empty** across the set (p1.2, p3, p5.1, all of p7, p10, p13, p20-21), so character voice and plot mechanics are essentially missing in this asset.
- **Clarity:** panel-to-panel action reads fine (fights and growth sequences are legible), but *who-is-who* is muddy: two protagonists, both ending in matching white/gold armor, plus a barely-explained male bystander who gets powered up (p11) and recurs at scale (p19-21).
- **narrative_arc:** cursed dark-haired woman drinks potion mid-confrontation → transforms into purple muscle-hero → brawls a Supergirl-type → cut to a second woman who transforms at home (white/gold) and powers up a male bystander → the two muscle-women clash in a canyon → white/gold force-feeds the protagonist another potion → escalating tit-for-tat potion-fueled growth (clothing/armor bursting, towering scale) → ends mid-escalation with the genie-lamp and a smug blonde (to be continued).

## Strengths to steal
- **Repeatable growth grid:** every transformation runs the same 4-panel ECU/CU progression (arms → abs/chest → glutes → legs: p2, p9, p15, p18). It's a reliable, fast template for dense zoom coverage — bottle it, but vary the framings between surges to avoid the recycling this book falls into.
- **Splash-caps every surge:** p3, p10, p16, p19 each end a transformation on a full-body reveal; the rhythm (grid of ECUs → big splash) gives growth a satisfying button. p19's EWS-with-tiny-man is the size-comparison masterclass.
- **SFX-as-sensation:** KZZZT/POLM pulsing through growth pages (p2, p9, p15, p18) carry the *feeling* of growing with zero dialogue; the brawl's WHAM/CRACK/WHUMP/KRAGSH/KRAKOOOM (p4, p13) carry kinetics. Steal the practice of lettering growth itself, not just impacts.
- **Dynamic clash staging:** diagonals + foreshortened fists + dutch tilts + reaction-intercut (p4, p12) and FG/BG scale-contrast (p11) are exactly the anti-flat playbook — concrete references for the pipeline's #1 visual failure.
- **The forced-trigger beat (p14):** resist → forced → pay off is the one place tension is built before release; a clean template for a delayed-gratification transformation.

## Weaknesses to avoid
- **No story spine / no climax (p21):** a potion arms-race that just escalates and stops. Visually loaded, dramatically inert — don't let "more growth" substitute for an actual arc and a real cliffhanger.
- **The flat domestic page (p7):** four eye-level mid-shots with slack faces. Even necessary tonal resets need a distance/angle change or a foreground element; this is the page that reads as default-flat.
- **Empty balloons everywhere:** dialogue is staged but unlettered across the set — a comic that bakes its words in (per the pipeline's dialogue rule) must not ship balloons with no text; here it leaves plot/character unreadable.
- **Identity confusion:** two protagonists, both ending in matching white/gold armor, introduced cold with no setup — the reader can't reliably tell them apart. Keep distinct silhouettes/palettes per character, especially during transformation.
- **Recycled framings:** the growth grids reuse the same four body-part shots each surge, and p13.3-13.4 are near-duplicate gloat MCUs. Effective devices still need variation so the back half doesn't feel like a loop.
