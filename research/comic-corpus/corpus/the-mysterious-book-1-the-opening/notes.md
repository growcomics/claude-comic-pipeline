## Growth-page accounting

  - trigger: P3 (book arrives), P22 (gauntlet donned)
  - early: P5 (first onset), P15 (round-2 onset)
  - mid: P6, P7, P8, P9, P10, P11, P16
  - peak: P12, P13, P23, P24
  - aftermath (NOT counted): P14, P17, P25
  - none (NOT counted): P1 cover, P2 setup, P4 buildup, P18–21 dialogue
- **Transformation scenes:**
  - **Round 1 — book round-robin reads, P5–14** (10 pages). Devices: multi-panel-progressive, clothing-destruction (P7 RIPPP → P11 RIIPPP burst → P12–13 shredded), sfx-driven, reaction-intercut, size-comparison (doorway P8), zoom-escalation (P11), slow-burn (10 pages), full-body-reveal (P14 front double-biceps). The book is physically passed person-to-person via SWIPE!/Swipe! handoffs (P4, P8) so each cast member grows in turn.
  - **Round 2 — apartment re-read, P15–17** (3 pages). Devices: sfx-driven (FWOOMP!/BAAAM!/BA-BUMP! on P16), clothing-destruction (P15 RIPP, P16 RIPP x2), size-comparison (CRUNK! crushed doorframe P17), multi-panel-progressive.
  - **Round 3 — golden-gauntlet power-up, P22–25** (4 pages). Devices: sfx-driven (GLOW/FWOOMP/BAAAM/SSSHHH), clothing-destruction, zoom-escalation, reaction-intercut, full-body-reveal (P25 caped-hero splash). A NEW device (the gauntlet) replaces the book so round 3 reads as a superhero origin, not a repeat.
- **Zoom coverage:** thin for a 60%-growth book. Only two true ECUs (P11 p3 awed eyes; P13 p3 muscle-creak KREEEEK detail). Most growth is shot MCU/MS. The transformations would hit harder with more ECU body-detail money-shots pushing in as size peaks.

## Camera
- **Distance spread across the book:** generally 2–4 distinct bands per page, but clustered in the MS/MCU mid-range. Widest pages: P3 (MS/MLS/WS/CU) and P13 (MCU/CU/ECU/MS). Narrowest: P6, P9, P10, P16, P19, P20 — all live almost entirely in MS/MCU. There is **no EWS/establishing shot anywhere** in 25 pages, so the spatial geography of the three locations (living room → gym → second apartment) is only implied.
- **Flat-panel hotspots:** the book is largely free of the flat-level failure mode — flat_panel_count is 0 on most pages. The exceptions are the early setup/conversation blocks: **P2 p2–4** (three flat-level talking-head panels, two near-identical) and scattered flat conversation beats (P3 p4, P4 p2/p4, P10 p2). Once growth starts (P5+), nearly every panel uses low-hero, foreshortening, scale-contrast, depth, or diagonal.
- **Best dynamic compositions to steal:**
  - **P13** — the whole page: a 7-panel cascade mixing MCU/CU/ECU/MS with low-hero and foreshortening, climaxing in CRASSHH.
  - **P11 p4** — low-hero MCU of the bursting top (RIIPPP x2) with an onlooker recoiling below = scale-contrast + clothing-destruction + peak face in one frame.
  - **P5 p3** — grower throws arms up center-frame while two friends recoil at smaller scale (clean big-subject / small-onlookers staging).
  - **P25** — closing splash on a low-hero diagonal flight; the single most cinematic frame in the issue.
  - **P17 p3–4** — using architecture (a crushed/too-small doorway, CRUNK!) as a fixed size reference for how big she's become.

## Expressions
- **How faces carry beats:** the transformation faces are the strength. Head-back open-mouth ecstasy/strain recurs through every growth round (P5 p2, P6 p1, P12 p4, P13 p1/p5, P16, P23 p4, P24 p5) and hits genuine **intensity 5** at the two climaxes (P13 p5, P24 p5). The argument page **P18** is carried entirely by escalating faces — shock (p1) rising to bared-teeth rage (p4) — a talk page that earns its keep on expression alone.
- **Dead-face / underplayed defects:** the **setup is too cool** — P2 p3–4 and several conversation panels sit at intensity 2 (mild/neutral) when a glowing magic book should register more curiosity or unease on the page-1–2 hook. A few **peak beats show no face at all** (P13 p3, P16 p3, P24 — recorded null), trading a money expression for an anatomy-only frame; acceptable as variety but a missed intensity opportunity at peak.

## Story & structure
- **Hook:** the cover (P1) makes a clear promise — three women reaching for a glowing book = "this book does something." But the actual hook is slow: P2 is a full page of (empty) conversation before the SHIING! catalyst on P3. The mystery-book premise is legible but the page-1 energy isn't cashed until P5.
- **Pacing:** front-loaded and back-loaded, saggy in the middle. Round 1 is a generous 10-page slow-burn (P5–14) — the book's best stretch. Then **P18–21 are four near-consecutive dialogue pages (~16 panels) with no growth**, a real lull between the round-2 peak (P17) and the gauntlet climax (P23). The climax (P22–25) is tight and strong.
- **Tease vs payoff:** this is mostly **dump-then-repeat**, not edge-and-delay. Each round triggers fast and pays off within a few pages (P5→ swelling within the same page; P15→P16 immediate). The closest thing to a tease is the gauntlet *plant* on P21 (the book reveals an illustrated glove) before the P23–24 payoff — one page of anticipation. For the redhead-houseguest edging structure, this comic is the **counter-example**: it never withholds, it just escalates device-by-device. The lesson to carry over is the *device escalation* (book → bigger book-reads → artifact), not the delay.
- **Cliffhanger / continuity:** clean. P25 resolves the arc into a two-woman superhero-team reveal and flies them off-frame with an explicit "to be continued..." box — a proper pull-forward to ch.2.
- **Dialogue & SFX:** SFX is the star and does the narrative heavy lifting — SHIING! (arrival), KRETHUNK!/RIPPP/RIIPPP (growth + clothing), GREEAGH/HNGGK (effort), KREEEEK→CRASSHH (strain→failure), FWOOMP!/BAAAM!/BA-BUMP! (impact pumps), GLOW/SSSHHH (artifact energy), HAHAHA!!! (triumph). Conversely the **dialogue is invisible in this scan — every balloon is empty** — so any plot/character nuance the script intended is lost to the corpus; the issue must be read as near-silent-with-SFX.
- **Clarity:** generally legible round-to-round, but **peak panels get muddy** — P12 p2–3 and P13 p7 cram 3–4 near-identical peak bodies into a frame where it's hard to track who is who or who is actively growing.

**Narrative arc:** five friends + a guy hang out → a glowing magic book materializes → each reads it in turn and muscle-grows (round-robin) → growth escalates to a peak gym session (bench shatters) → scene resets to an apartment, more reading + an argument → the book yields a golden gauntlet → two women power up in a glowing climax → they emerge as caped muscle-heroines and fly off (to be continued).

## Strengths to steal
- **P13's pure-SFX growth cascade** (HAH/HAH → KREEEEK → HNGGK → CRASSHH) carries strain to structural failure with no dialogue — sound design alone sells the sensation. Reproduce this: let SFX, not balloons, narrate a peak.
- **Low-hero + foreshortening on the grower, every time** (P5, P6, P11, P12, P13, P16, P23, P24) makes the transforming body tower over the lens — the direct antidote to the pipeline's flat-level mid-shot default.
- **The book as a round-robin engine** (SWIPE! handoffs P4/P8 passing it to a new reader each time) turns one transformation into a 10-page slow-burn across the whole cast. A reusable structure for spreading growth over pages.
- **Device escalation, not just size escalation:** book-reads → golden gauntlet (P22–24) with energy-ribbon FX. Changing the *mechanism* keeps a long transformation sequence visually fresh.
- **A clothing-destruction ladder:** single RIPPP (P7) → RIIPPP x2 burst (P11) → shredded-to-nude peak (P12–13). Stage tears as a rising series so garments visibly lose the race to the muscle.
- **Architecture as size reference** (P8 doorway, P17 CRUNK! crushed doorframe) — cheap, legible way to register "she's huge now" without a second figure.
- **A real cliffhanger splash** (P25): full-body-reveal + diagonal flight + "to be continued." Model for closing a chapter on a forward pull.

## Weaknesses to avoid
- **Don't ship pages of empty conversation.** P2 and P18–21 are talk-heavy with no readable dialogue in the scan and no camera movement; the social beats land as dead air. If lettering is deferred, the visuals must still carry the page — these don't.
- **Don't let the middle third stall.** Four consecutive dialogue pages (P18–21, ~16 panels) between two growth peaks kills momentum. Keep growth or escalation present every 1–2 pages in an FMG book.
- **Don't crowd the money panels.** P12 p2–3 and P13 p7 pile 3–4 near-identical peak bodies into one frame and lose clarity. Isolate the grower at the peak so the reader can read the change.
- **Don't under-cover with zoom.** For a 60%-growth book there are only two ECUs in 25 pages. Add ECU body-detail money-shots that push in *as* size peaks — the rubric's zoom-escalation is the most underused device here.
- **Don't underplay the hook.** P1–2 setup faces sit at intensity 2; a magic-book mystery should register curiosity/unease earlier to grab the reader before P5.
- **Avoid composition formula fatigue.** The "grower head-back + onlooker reaching in at smaller scale" staging recurs across all three rounds (P5/P6/P11/P16/P20/P23) — effective once, formulaic by the third use. Vary the onlooker framing.
- **Don't introduce a character thread and drop it.** The man transforms on P10 then is demoted to spotter/comic relief (P12–14) with no payoff. Either pay it off or don't plant it.
- **Add at least one EWS per location.** With no establishing wide anywhere, the three settings never read as real spaces.
