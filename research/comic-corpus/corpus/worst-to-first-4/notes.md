## Camera
- **Distance spread across the book:** wide. The book ranges from EWS corridor/silhouette insets (P6.1, P16.2, P17.1, P18.2) through full WS reveals down to body-part ECUs (P2.3, P7.3, P12.3, P15.2). Most pages hit 3-4 distinct distance bands (P6 and P14-15 hit 4). Only P11 is narrow (MS + a row of CU grid tiles, spread=2).
- **Flat-panel hotspots:** P10 panels 2-3 (level-eyeline talking-heads tension cuts), and the **P11 four-tile reaction grid** (all level eyelines by design). P3.2, P8.4, P9.2 are isolated flat CUs. None are egregious, but the P10/P11 cluster is where the camera goes static.
- **Best dynamic compositions to steal:**
  - **P12** — MS (SHI-NG) → CU (RAAH/BAAM) → ECU (BULGE) zoom-escalation push-in on an erupting chest. The template for a one-page growth surge.
  - **P4.4** — wide back-spread with arms flung out and a panel-spanning RIII-IIP; preceded by a matched OTS progressive pair (P4.1→4.2).
  - **P5.1** — double-biceps flex framed *inside* a second character's massive FG thighs: scale-contrast + foreshortening in one shot.
  - **P6.1 + P6.2** — EWS winged-silhouette corridor inset cutting to a low-hero villain reveal; great menace-by-scale.
  - **P16.1 → P16.2** — low-hero hero splash (DUN DUN DUUN) dropping to an EWS horizon silhouette (SHI-NG); strong scale move.
  - **P14** — full magical-girl montage (dutch spin tile → growth detail → eyes ECU → full-body WS reveal → flight WS with ground-crater); the most cinematic page.

## Expressions
- Faces carry the *strain/rage/ecstasy* beats well when present: **P2.1** head-back ecstasy (5), **P2.4** sweat-strain (5), **P8.3** fanged BOOOOM rage (5), **P10.1** bloodied glare (5), **P12.2** roar (5), **P6.2** villain smug (4), **P9.2** cocky grin (4). When this comic shows a face on a growth beat, it usually commits.
- **Dead-face / missing-face defects:** the problem is *absence*, not blankness. Entire growth money-shots are face-less — **all of P4** (back-burst), **P5.3** (calf ECU), **P7.3** (abs ECU), most of **P15**'s feat tiles. P5.2 has a turned/tiny face on a peak flex (intensity ~1). The niche rewards a strain/ecstasy face on these very panels; leaving them faceless is the recurring miss.
- Net: when a face is in frame it sells the moment (justifies a 3, leaning 4 on faces shown), but coverage is inconsistent because so many panels crop the head out.

## Story & structure
- **Hook:** instant but contextless. Page 1 opens cold mid-transformation (girl chugging a drink, back already bulging, KRIIIIINNGG) — as Part 4 it assumes you're caught up. Visually grabby, narratively opaque.
- **Pacing:** front-to-back wall-to-wall growth. Almost no setup/denouement breathing room; it's transformation after transformation. Drags only in the P10 roster pause and the P11 grid; rushes the dark-haired (P5) and mohawk (P8) characters straight to peak with little stage coverage.
- **Tease vs payoff:** minimal tease — it mostly *dumps*. Each character strains→rips→reveals within 1-2 pages and pays off immediately; little anticipation is banked before release. **Relevance to the redhead-houseguest edging structure: this comic is the opposite model** — pure rapid release with no delay. Useful as a negative reference for "how NOT to pace if you want sustained edging." The lone exception is the blonde's slower magical-girl build (P13→14), which earns its reveal more.
- **Cliffhanger / continuity:** ends *soft*. P18's feather + angel-wing + serene face seeds a new angelic power for the next part but lands on calm, not a hard pull-forward; the introduced antagonist (P6/P9 bat-wing blonde) never gets a paid-off confrontation on these pages.
- **Dialogue & SFX:** SFX do ~all the narrative lifting and do it well (panel-spanning RIII-IIP/BOOOOM/BAAM/CRA-CK!/DUN DUN DUUN plus live-growth stamps BULGE/PUMP/TIGHTEN/CREAAAK). **Dialogue is entirely absent — every balloon is blank** (un-lettered raw), which is the single biggest structural hole: motivation, rivalry, and stakes are invisible.
- **Clarity:** panel-to-panel growth is legible (you always know a body is getting bigger), but *who* is transforming and *why* is not — the rotating cast with drifting hair/identity and zero captions makes the throughline hard to follow.

**narrative_arc:** in-medias-res locker-room power-up → pink/red/dark-haired teammates each muscle-transform one after another → blonde antagonist (bat-wing motif) menaces → cast powers up further off green energy/orb → magical-girl-style peak transformations (blonde, red-ponytail) → caped muscle-heroes assemble → angelic feather denouement (to be continued).

## Strengths to steal
- **Growth-forward cadence with a rotating cast** (P1-5, P7-8, P11-16): keep transformation on-page almost continuously and rotate *which* character is growing so the book never cools off. This is the slow-burn-many-characters rhythm the pipeline wants.
- **The P12 zoom push-in template:** MS surge → CU strain → ECU body-part BULGE, with the same furious face held through all three. Reproducible as a stock 3-panel growth beat.
- **P4 progressive + rip:** matched OTS pair escalating one body part, then a wide back-spread, then a single panel-spanning RIII-IIP for the clothing-destruction climax. Clean stage-to-stage staging.
- **SFX as the engine:** oversized panel-spanning lettering for the big beats + small "live" stamps (BULGE, PUMP, CREAAAK, TIGHTEN) layered onto growth panels — this carries sensation with zero dialogue. (Note: do it *in addition to* dialogue, not instead of.)
- **Per-character transformation grammar:** giving the blonde a magical-girl ribbon/sparkle build (P13-14) distinct from the others' raw shirt-bursts keeps the repeated power-ups from feeling identical.
- **Scale language:** low-hero + FG-limb foreshortening to make the muscle-women tower (P5.1, P6.2, P12, P16.1), and EWS silhouette insets for epic scale-contrast (P6.1, P16.2, P17.1).

## Weaknesses to avoid
- **Never ship with empty dialogue balloons.** P1/P2/P3/P6/P7/P9/P10/P11/P14/P17/P18 all have blank balloons — the plot is unreadable. Bake the lettering (this is exactly the "bake dialogue into every panel" lesson; SFX alone is not a story).
- **Don't crop the face out of the money-shots.** P4's entire back-burst, P5.3, P7.3, P15's feat tiles carry no strain/ecstasy face — the niche's highest-value real estate is left emotionless. Keep a reacting face in frame (or inset) on peak-growth panels.
- **Lock cast identity / hair across panels.** Characters swap focus with no captions and hair-identity drifts (red-ponytail vs red-mohawk vs red-and-black; P8 vs P12 vs P18 may or may not be one person) — the reader loses track of who's who. Use captions and a consistent ref per character.
- **Avoid flat-level talking-heads stacks.** P10.2-3 and the P11 four-tile grid pile up same-plane eye-level CUs; vary the angle/scale even on reaction cuts.
- **Don't re-run the same beat without escalation.** "Strain → shirt rips → reveal" repeats ~5x with little rising stakes between transformations and no antagonist payoff — structure the sequence so each transformation tops the last, and pay off the threat you introduced (the P6/P9 villain).
- **Don't fizzle the ending.** P18's calm angel-wing close is a soft non-cliffhanger after 17 pages of escalation; end on a hard pull-forward for a serialized book.
