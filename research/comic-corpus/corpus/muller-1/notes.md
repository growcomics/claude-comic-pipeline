- Transformation scenes:
  - **early-growth-montage (p6, p8, p9)** — devices: `size-comparison` (p8p1 glute comparison), `slow-burn`, `sfx-driven` (GRUNT p6), `reaction-intercut`. Subtle firming, first display.
  - **ladder-accident-trigger (p11–12)** — devices: `sfx-driven` (CRASH p11 / THUD p12). The catalyst: doused in chemical mid-fall, emerges unhurt and stronger.
  - **strength-reveal-and-flex (p13–15)** — devices: `full-body-reveal` (p14 mirror flex splash), `size-comparison` (p15p2 vs patrons), `reaction-intercut` (onlooker p15p3), `sfx-driven` (KRAK-crush p13p3), `zoom-escalation` (p13 tightens from worm-WS to MS strength tests). The payoff stretch.
- Zoom coverage: **thin for an FMG book.** True body-part ECUs are essentially absent — the only ECUs in the book are of phone/tablet *screens* (p10p4, p19p3), not growing muscle. Growth is carried at MS/WS with low-hero + scale-contrast rather than tight detail. Best body-focus shots are MS flex/lift money panels (p10p2, p13p2-3, p14, p15p3), not ECUs.

## Camera
- **Distance spread across the book:** healthy overall — every band WS→ECU appears. Best single-page spread is **p10 (WS→ECU, distance_spread=4)**. Most pages hit 2–3 distinct distances.
- **Flat-panel hotspots:** the front-loaded domestic setup — **p1 (p1p2, p1p4, p1p5), p2 (p2p1, p2p3)** — and the dosing pages **p17 (p17p2-3)** are level-eyeline mid/close shots that sit still. The connective tissue is where the camera dies.
- **Best dynamic compositions to STEAL:**
  - **p14** — near-splash mirror double-biceps: big-FG muscular back vs the flexing reflection, low-hero. The reference composition for a full-body muscle reveal.
  - **p11p3-4** — dutch/diagonal CRASH + inverted fall inside a yellow radial impact burst, legs foreshortened to camera. Proves the artist can stage real kinetic energy.
  - **p13p1** — worm/low angle up at her towering over the fallen ladder, sneakers thrust at camera.
  - **p8p1** — low-angle glutes FG vs protagonist standing: a clean size-comparison built straight into the staging.
  - **p4p1 / p15p2** — big-FG protagonist with smaller fit figures in lit depth; recurring, effective scale-contrast.

## Expressions
- Faces **carry the beat where it counts on the spectacle**: awe/shock on the strength reveal (p13p1-2, intensity 4), open-mouth ecstasy in the intimate scenes (p9p1, p18p2, intensity 4), and a genuinely sinister glowing-eye smirk on the dosing pages (p17p2, p17p5, p19p2) — that recurring hypnotic-glow motif is the book's strongest single expression device.
- **Dead-face defects** drag the average down: the husband and protagonist run flat-neutral through the setup and plot connective tissue — **p1p2, p1p4** (phone-scrolling, intensity 1), **p2p3** (dishes, intensity 1), **p10p5** (faceless silhouette, fine), **p19p3** (faceless screen ECU). The marriage is established without anyone emoting, so the reader feels nothing until the gym/strength beats arrive.
- Verdict: expressive on the money beats, emotionless on the glue. A pipeline lesson: the *connective* panels need acting too, not just the splash.

## Story & structure
- **Hook:** slow. P1 is competent domestic table-setting (bored wife, distracted phone-husband) with the chemical motif quietly planted (green glow, p1p3) — but a reader skimming would not know this is an FMG book until **p6** (first firming) and would not get a real payoff until **p13–14**. The true hook (she's a secret chemist drugging him) only lands around p16–17.
- **Pacing:** lopsided. Setup/buildup runs long (p1–5, five pages before any change), the growth payoff is a tight, strong burst (p11–15), and then the book **stalls** — four of the last five pages (p16–19) are brewing/dosing/scheming with no active growth, exactly when the transformed body should be driving momentum.
- **Tease vs payoff:** this is a *delayed-release* structure and the tease half works (slow firming p6→p8→p9, then the accident). Directly relevant to the redhead-houseguest edging brief: Muller proves you can hold a transformation across ~10 pages and still land it — BUT it fumbles the release by never showing growth *in the act*. The payoff is "cut to: now she's bigger," not a depicted swell. The edge is built; the climax is elided. Lesson: delay is fine, but the release beat must be *on-panel and progressive*, or the held tension deflates.
- **Cliffhanger / continuity:** ends on a pull-forward (p20: a new brunette arrives by red sports car holding the same chemical spray bottle) — good franchise hook linking her to the product, but anticlimactic: the final splash real estate is spent introducing an *unestablished* face instead of paying off the protagonist.
- **Dialogue & SFX:** lettering is **absent** in the supplied files — nearly every speech/thought balloon and caption box is blank (p1p1 caption, p19p1, p20p1). The book therefore reads almost silent and leans entirely on visuals + *environmental/impact* SFX, which do real work: GYM (p3), WACH SEI (p4), GRUNT (p6), HUFF HUFF (p10p2), MYO… (p10p1), CRASH (p11), THUD (p12), KRAK-crush (p13p3), POUR (p16), recurring ZZ dosing-spark (p17, p19). SFX carry the sensation; dialogue is a hole.
- **Clarity:** mostly legible panel-to-panel, but the missing lettering creates ambiguity at the seams — e.g., **p7** (fancy restaurant) reads as either a memory/flashback or present-day date with no caption to disambiguate, and the protagonist's *motive* (why she's dosing him) is unstated.

**Narrative arc:** bored housewife cooks for distracted husband → secretly brews a green glowing chemical → starts going to the gym → falls off a ladder unhurt and emerges suddenly muscular → slow-burn growth across gym + mirror-flex reveals while dosing husband's food/wine → intimate scenes punctuate → ends on a new brunette arriving in a sports car with her own spray bottle (to be continued).

## Strengths to steal
- **P14 mirror-flex near-splash** — low-hero, big-FG muscular back vs the beaming flexing reflection. Back-vs-reflection *doubles* the reveal in one frame; this is the template for a full-body muscle money page.
- **P11p3-4 trigger action** — dutch/diagonal CRASH + inverted fall in a radial impact burst with foreshortened legs. Steal the radial-burst + foreshortening for any catalytic/impact beat.
- **P13 whole page** — every panel pairs a dynamic angle with a high-intensity face (worm-angle full body, metal-crush + impact star + grin, low-hero power pose). The standard to hit: angle AND expression together, not one or the other.
- **Low-hero + scale-contrast as a size-signal** — used repeatedly (p4p1, p8p1, p10p2, p15p2-3) to make her physically dominate normal-sized patrons. Clean, reproducible dominance device.
- **Glowing-eye motif** (p17p2, p17p5, p19p2) — a cheap, repeatable expression beat that injects menace/agency and ties the dosing thread together.
- **SFX-as-sensation** — with dialogue absent, GRUNT/HUFF/CRASH/THUD/KRAK still sell effort and impact. Confirms SFX can carry growth/strength beats solo (relevant to the user's silent-panel work).

## Weaknesses to avoid
- **Zero multi-panel-progressive growth** — the gold-standard device the pipeline wants most is entirely missing. Every stage-up (p6→p8→p13/14→p15) is a single "now she's bigger" reveal; no body part is ever shown growing stage→stage→stage across adjacent panels. The growth happens *between* pages, never on one.
- **No clothing-destruction beat** — the torn waistband (p14) is a static aftermath detail, not a garment bursting *during* growth. A glaring miss given all the heavy-lifting scenes that should pop seams.
- **Thin body-part ECU coverage** — the only ECUs are of phone/tablet *screens* (p10p4, p19p3), never of growing/flexing muscle. An FMG book should be putting ECUs on the muscle, not the messaging app.
- **Front-loaded flatness + dead faces** — p1–2 are static flat-level mid-shots of phone-scrolling with intensity-1 neutral faces (p1p2, p1p4, p2p3); five pages elapse before any change. Don't open this slow or this still.
- **Momentum stall at peak transformation** — p16–19 (4 of the last 5 pages) are brewing/dosing/scheming with no active growth, killing momentum right when the body is most transformed and should be driving the issue.
- **Anticlimactic ending** — p20 spends the closing splash on a brand-new, unestablished brunette instead of the protagonist. Hook forward, yes — but don't end an issue on a face the reader has no investment in.
- **Absent lettering** — blank balloons/captions throughout (p1p1, p19p1, p20p1) leave motive and chronology ambiguous (e.g., p7 memory-vs-present). Bake the dialogue in; don't ship a transformation whose *why* is unstated.
