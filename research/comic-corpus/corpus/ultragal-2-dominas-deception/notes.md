## Camera
- **Distance spread across the book:** strong overall. Dynamic pages with spread≥3: p2, p6, p8, p9, p12, p13, p14, p17, p21. The robot fight (p8) and debut flight (p6) are the most cinematically varied — EWS aerials, ECUs, low-hero landings all on one page.
- **Flat-panel hotspots:** essentially none flagged `flat-level`. The real soft spots are *distance monotony*, not flatness: **p18** is four consecutive MS panels (spread=1), and **p10** and **p18** lean on MS/MCU posturing where the action pages would have varied scale. The heroine-vs-heroine brawls (p9-12, p17-20) are consistently less varied than the robot/flight material.
- **Best dynamic compositions to steal:**
  - **p7** — three-layer depth/scale-contrast: huge foreground hero mass, midground villain, towering background robots. The single best staging in the issue.
  - **p5 / p16** — worm's-eye low-hero full-body reveal splashes with converging buildings (p5) / cracked-city depth (p16).
  - **p6 panel1** — SWOOOOSH push-in with a foreshortened punch-arm; **p6 panel2** — EWS aerial for scale relief between action beats.
  - **p3** — the left full-figure ecstasy panel paired with a right-side stacked column of progressive body-part ECUs: a clean, reusable transformation-page template.

## Expressions
- **Where faces carry the beat:** the Domina transformation is the high-water mark — **p13 panel3** and **p14 panel2** are peak (intensity-5) ecstasy/rage power-high faces, and **p13 panel2** cuts to Ultragal's wide-eyed hand-to-mouth shock (the best reaction beat in the book). The fights also stay expressive: **p9 panel3** and **p11 panels1-2** are intensity-4 rage CUs; the climax (p20) keeps strained/determined faces throughout.
- **Dead-face defects (the call-out):** **Ultragal's own transformation goes faceless at its peak** — every growth ECU on **p4** (chest/shoulders/arms) and all four ECUs on **p3** are intensity-0. The marquee hero moment is sold entirely by SFX + burst-lines while the face is out of frame. Same pattern on **p15 panels3-5** (Domina's body ECUs). The studio clearly *can* put a screaming face on a growth beat (it does so for Domina on p13-14 and on the p16 splash) — it just didn't do it for the hero. A few inset reaction cuts during the body ECUs would close the gap cheaply.

## Story & structure
- **Hook:** very fast. Ordinary glasses-wearing redhead (Mira) on p2, transformation already underway on p3 — the issue is in active growth by its second story page. Page-1 cover promises a flying muscle-hero and the book delivers her by p5.
- **Pacing:** front-loaded and brisk. Two transformation bursts (p3-4, p13-16) bracket a long fight middle. Almost no connective/quiet tissue — only p2 is true setup. The risk is the **two brawls (p9-12, p17-20) being structurally repetitive** ("rivals trade blows"); the Domina TF in the middle is the main thing differentiating them.
- **Tease vs payoff:** more *dump* than *tease* on the first transformation (Ultragal changes the moment we meet her, no anticipation built). The **better-handled delay** is structural: the *title's* "deception" is teased by Domina's confident posturing (p7, p10) and her staged "defeat" (p11 high-vuln) before the reversal — payoff is withheld until p13's transformation and finally the p21 mastermind reveal. Useful reference for the redhead-houseguest edging structure: the *meta* tease (who is Domina really / what is the deception) is paid off late even though the *physical* growth is paid off early.
- **Cliffhanger / continuity:** ends on a genuine pull-forward. p21 intercuts Ultragal's hollow aerial "victory" with a lab-coated mastermind holding a control device over the beaten Domina-puppet, buttoning on the villain's smug raised finger — "to be continued," not a clean KO.
- **Dialogue & SFX:** SFX are the workhorse and they're good — BABAAAM/BAAAM (growth), RIIIP/KRRRK (cloth), KYZZZ (electric power-up), WHAAAM/KRUUNK/CRAASSHH/KRZAARK/TWHAACK/WHUUMP (combat). **Dialogue, however, is entirely absent — every speech balloon on every page is left EMPTY.** Whether that's an un-lettered scan or a deliberate choice, the practical effect is the same: motivation, the rivalry's cause, and the lab-coat reveal must be inferred. SFX carry sensation well; the empty balloons hurt clarity and stakes.
- **Clarity:** action-to-action legibility is good (you always know who hit whom), but *narrative* clarity is weak — without dialogue it's ambiguous that the "Domina" Ultragal fought is a controlled decoy and that the lab-coat woman is the real antagonist; the reader reconstructs the twist from visual cues alone.

**Narrative arc:** ordinary redhead walks the city → triggers a secret transformation into muscle-hero Ultragal → flies off and smashes attacking robots → meets a rival heroine (Domina) → they brawl → Domina reveals her own deception and explosively muscle-transforms to match → escalating size-vs-size duel → Ultragal appears to win, but the twist reveals a lab-coated mastermind controlling the fight, leaving Ultragal manipulated (to be continued).

## Strengths to steal
- **Build two transformations, and make the second one face-forward.** Domina's TF (p13-16) outperforms the hero's because it *opens on expression* (intensity-5 ecstasy/rage CUs) and cuts to an onlooker's shock (p13 panel2) before showing mass. Lead growth with the face + a reaction-intercut, then deliver the ECUs.
- **The stacked progressive-ECU column.** p3's layout — one full-figure reaction panel beside a vertical stack of chest→abs→glutes ECUs with RIIIP/KRRRK — is a directly reusable "transformation page" template; p15 reruns it for clothing-destruction (chest→legs).
- **Mirror full-body reveals as scene buttons.** p5 and p16 are matched worm's-eye low-hero splashes paying off each TF; p16 (max muscle + max face together) is the model — always pair the size payoff with a peak expression.
- **Three-layer depth staging (p7).** Big foreground hero mass / midground figure / tall background threat = instant scale and stakes; steal this for any "hero confronts a threat" reveal.
- **Let SFX carry growth sensation.** BABAAAM→RIIIP→KYZZZ escalation conveys swelling, tearing, and surging power with zero dialogue — exactly the sfx-driven device the pipeline should reach for on growth beats.
- **Keep travel/transit pages kinetic (p6).** A non-growth flight beat stays cinematic via SWOOOOSH push-in → EWS aerial → ZOOM dive → FWAAM landing; don't let connective pages go flat.

## Weaknesses to avoid
- **Don't let the hero's growth go faceless.** Ultragal's peak (p4 chest/shoulders/arms ECUs, all intensity-0; p3 four ECUs all 0) sells the money moment with SFX alone. When the body fills frame, cut an inset of the face — the studio already proves it works for Domina.
- **Don't ship empty dialogue balloons.** Every balloon in the issue is blank (p2-p21); stakes, the rivalry's cause, and the p21 deception are left to inference. Bake at least minimal lettering so the twist reads (per the studio's own "bake dialogue into every panel" rule).
- **Avoid distance monotony in two-character brawls.** p18 is four straight MS panels; p10 flattens to MS/MCU posturing. The heroine-vs-heroine fights are markedly less varied than the robot/flight pages — vary shot distance the way p8/p17 do.
- **Don't run two near-identical fight blocks.** p9-12 and p17-20 are both "rivals trade blows" with similar framing; only the mid-book TF differentiates them. Give the second brawl a distinct spatial idea (new location, new size dynamic foregrounded) so it doesn't read as padding before the cliffhanger.
- **Don't under-build setup if you want the twist to land.** One setup page (p2) is great for pace but leaves no room to plant who Domina is, so the p21 mastermind reveal has to do all its work visually — a single earlier beat establishing the lab-coat antagonist would make the deception pay off cleanly.
