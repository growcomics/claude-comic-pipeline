# FMG Anatomy Guide — Prompt Reference

This file distills the FMG Anatomy Guide (General) into prompt-friendly language for Nano Banana panel prompts. Use it when describing character physiques, poses, and muscle detail in `panels.json`.

The core philosophy: **exaggerated muscles, anatomically accurate placement, feminine figure always preserved.**

---

## 1. Overall Figure / Shape

### DO
- Maintain an **hourglass figure** at every muscle size — shoulders and hips should be roughly equal width, with a visible waist taper between them
- Use **curves for every contour** — round, flowing lines define the entire body shape
- Keep the figure **balanced front-to-back**: large breasts in front, large glutes behind, creating a dramatic S-curve in side profile

### DON'T
- Never let the figure become a **reverse triangle** (shoulders much wider than hips) — that reads as masculine
- Never use **triangular or angular shapes** for the body outline — hard edges and sharp angles kill the feminine read
- Never flatten the waist — even at extreme muscle sizes, the waist must taper inward

### Prompt language
> "hourglass figure, equal shoulder-to-hip ratio, flowing curved contours, dramatic waist taper"

---

## 2. Muscle Size — What Goes Big vs. What Stays Small

This is the defining contrast that makes FMG work. Certain body parts are exaggerated massively; others stay deliberately small and feminine. The size differential is what sells the fantasy.

### GO BIG (exaggerate these)
- Biceps
- Triceps
- Thighs (quads and hamstrings)
- Calves
- Lats
- Breasts
- Butt/glutes

### KEEP SMALL / FEMININE (never enlarge these)
- Head
- Hands
- Traps (keep low, not bulging up toward ears)
- Shoulders (keep rounded, not boulder-like)
- Knees
- Feet

### Prompt language
> "massively enlarged biceps and thighs contrasted against a small feminine head and delicate hands"
>
> "enormous muscles but small traps and narrow shoulders preserving a feminine frame"

## 3. Arms

### Muscle Groups (anatomically accurate, exaggerated scale)
The arm consists of overlapping shapes: deltoid cap on top, massive biceps brachii on front, triceps brachii on back, coracobrachialis and teres major where arm meets torso, and pectoralis major wrapping underneath. Each muscle has a **clear, distinct shape** — they read as separate rounded masses, not one blended blob.

### Shape Flow
- **Large muscles are surrounded by smaller, more detailed muscles** — the smaller muscles act as a frame that makes the big muscles look even bigger
- Each muscle should read as its own **distinct rounded shape** (like overlapping circles/ovals of different sizes)

### FMG Arms vs Normal Arms
- Normal arms: muscles only visible when **flexed**, smooth when relaxed
- FMG arms: muscles are **always visible** even when relaxed — defined, separated, detailed in every pose
- FMG arms when flexed show **more detail and separation** than normal flexed arms

### Forearm Shape
- **DON'T**: Draw the forearm as a single round mass tapering to the wrist — this looks like a "drumstick"
- **DO**: Give the forearm **flowing contours** with visible muscle groups that create an organic, curved taper
- **DON'T**: Make the wrist too long — keep it compact
- **DO**: Let the forearm muscles flow smoothly into a short wrist

### Prompt language
> "massively defined arms with each muscle group clearly separated — enormous round biceps, thick horseshoe triceps, detailed deltoid cap, all muscles visible even in relaxed pose"
>
> "forearms with flowing muscular contours tapering to compact feminine wrists, not drumstick-shaped"

## 4. Breasts

### Shape Rules
- **Round, not teardrop** — FMG breasts are drawn as near-perfect spheres
- They **ignore gravity** — always sitting high and full, as if permanently pushed up
- "The rounder the better" — avoid any drooping, sagging, or teardrop shape
- Think: always wearing an invisible push-up bra

### Normal vs FMG Comparison
- Normal breasts: teardrop-shaped, affected by gravity, hang lower
- FMG breasts: round-shaped, gravity-defying, always high and full on the chest

### Prompt language
> "large perfectly round breasts sitting high on the chest, gravity-defying, full spherical shape"
>
> "breasts are round not teardrop, pushed up and prominent against the muscular chest"

## 5. Abs

### Shape and Form
Key muscle groups in the torso: sternocleidomastoid (neck), trapezius, deltoid, pectoralis major, latissimus dorsi, serratus anterior, rectus abdominis, external oblique (thoracic portion and flank pad portion), pyramidalis.

### Drawing Rules
- Abs are **square-shaped but with smooth, rounded surfaces** — think bread buns, not ice cubes
- **Never blocky with hard edges** — each ab segment has soft, pillowy curves
- From the side, each ab should have a subtle **B-shape** curve, not a flat face
- The obliques (normally not visible at lower body fat) are drawn anyway to **preserve the feminine waist curve** — this is the hourglass shape showing through even in the midsection

### V-Taper and Proportions
- In heroic anatomy, the abs form an **upright V shape** — wider at the top (under the chest), tapering narrower toward the lower abs
- **DON'T** draw an inverted V (wider at bottom, narrower at top) — this is a common mistake that looks anatomically wrong
- **Lower abs are smaller** than upper abs due to this V-taper. The lowest ab segments should **recede and taper** into the mons pubis area, not end abruptly at the same size as the upper abs
- **Ab width matters**: the abs should stretch laterally to about **50% of the breast width**. Too narrow and they look pinched; this is one of the most common proportion errors
- In real anatomy the rectus abdominis runs roughly straight (parallel sides), but heroic/FMG anatomy **exaggerates the V-taper** for dramatic effect

### Shading and Lighting
- Each ab segment should be **lit from above** — highlight on the top surface, shadow underneath. This is the standard for top-lit scenes and makes abs look hard and defined
- **DON'T** reverse the shading (shadow on top, highlight below) — this makes abs look flabby and soft instead of firm
- **Lighting must be consistent across the whole torso** — if the breasts have highlights on top (from overhead light), the abs must also have highlights on top. Mismatched lighting between breasts and abs is a dead giveaway of bad shading
- Think of each ab as a small sphere: incident highlight on the surface facing the light, core shadow on the opposite side, cast shadow underneath where it meets the next ab segment

### Oblique Detail Priority
- The external oblique has two regions: the **thoracic portion** (upper, near the ribs) and the **flank pad portion** (lower, near the hips)
- The thoracic portion should have **MORE detail** — it's more visible and creates the criss-cross side-ab pattern
- The flank pad portion should have **LESS detail** — it's further from the viewer's focus and over-detailing it while under-detailing the thoracic portion inverts the visual hierarchy
- The criss-crossing oblique lines **stop at around the lats** — they don't wrap all the way around the body

### Perspective Consistency
- When the body is at a **3/4 angle**, the abs MUST follow that same angle — they sit on the same plane as the torso
- **DON'T** draw abs facing straight-on when the rest of the body is turned — this is a perspective break that makes the torso look disconnected
- In 3/4 view, the near-side abs appear wider and more foreshortened, the far-side abs appear narrower and partially hidden

### Lats
- Hinting at lat spread behind the torso gives a **more balanced, powerful look**
- Without lats hinted, the upper body can feel top-heavy or unanchored
- With lats, the figure widens slightly at the ribcage, creating better flow from shoulders → waist → hips

### Ab Shape Flow
- **Avoid symmetry** — stiff, perfectly mirrored abs feel lifeless
- Add **slight asymmetry** to ab shapes — one side slightly different from the other
- Abs should have **feminine curves** in their flow, following the body's natural S-curve
- Ab shapes **change based on pose and arm position**:
  - Neutral pose: abs are their natural rounded rectangle shape
  - Arms raised or turned: abs compress, tilt, and squeeze on one side
  - Extreme poses: ab shapes become more diamond/rhombus-like as they're compressed
- Adding **explosion/burst effects** around the abs during growth sequences emphasizes the transformation moment

### Prompt language
> "defined six-pack abs in an upright V-taper, wider under the chest and narrowing toward the lower abs, each segment smooth and rounded like bread buns, not blocky or hard-edged"
>
> "abs stretching to half the breast width, with visible obliques preserving the feminine waist curve, lower abs tapering and receding smoothly"
>
> "top-lit abs with highlights on the upper surface of each segment and shadows underneath, matching the overhead lighting on the breasts, firm and defined not flabby"
>
> "abs with slight asymmetry following the body's natural curve, hinted lat spread giving a balanced powerful figure, detailed thoracic obliques with simpler flank pads"

### QA Checklist — Abs-Specific Failures
Use this when reviewing generated output. These are the most common abs errors:

| Check | What to look for | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| V-taper direction | Abs should be wider at top, narrower at bottom — NOT inverted | Add "upright V-taper" to prompt |
| Shading direction | Highlight on top of each ab, shadow underneath — NOT reversed | Specify "top-lit abs, highlight on upper surface" |
| Lighting consistency | Ab highlights must match breast highlights (same light source) | Add "consistent overhead lighting across torso" |
| Ab width | Should be ~50% of breast width — not pinched narrow | Add "abs stretching to half the breast width" |
| Lower ab termination | Lowest abs should taper and recede, not end abruptly | Add "lower abs tapering smoothly into the pelvis" |
| Oblique detail | Thoracic portion (upper) = more detail, flank pad (lower) = less | Specify "detailed upper obliques, simpler lower flanks" |
| 3/4 perspective | Abs must follow the same angle as the body, not face straight-on | Add "abs following the 3/4 body angle" in angled shots |
| Blocky feel | Shading too uniform or hard-edged makes abs look like blocks | Add "smooth rounded surfaces, bread-bun shapes" |

## 6. Butt / Glutes

### Proportion Rules
- **Reverse triangle (shoulders wider than butt) = masculine** — AVOID
- **Hourglass (shoulders equal to butt)** = ideal FMG proportions
- **Triangle (butt wider than shoulders) = extra feminine** — also acceptable, even desirable
- The butt should **balance the breast size** — large breasts need a proportionally large butt to maintain the S-curve in side profile

### Shape
- Hips can **merge with the butt into one flowing shape** — no hard line separating hip from glute
- The glutes should be round and full, maintaining the curved-shapes-only rule

### Prompt language
> "large round glutes balancing the breast size, hourglass proportions with butt at least as wide as shoulders"
>
> "full rounded hips merging seamlessly into prominent glutes, feminine triangle proportions"
>
> "butt and hips forming one flowing curved shape, proportionally large to balance the upper body"

## 7. Legs

### Muscle Groups (anatomically accurate, exaggerated)
Front: rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, sartorius, tensor fasciae latae.
Back: biceps femoris, semitendinosus, semimembranosus, gracilis.
Lower: gastrocnemius, soleus, tibialis anterior, peroneus longus/brevis, extensor digitorum longus.
Connective: iliotibial tract, achilles tendon.

### Shape Flow — The Critical Rule
- **DON'T**: Make the inner and outer leg contours mirror each other (same shape on both sides) — this creates a "drumstick" look
- **DO**: Use **different shapes on the inner vs outer side** of each leg — the quad sweep on the outside should be a different curve than the adductor/hamstring shape on the inside
- The shapes should **complement each other asymmetrically** — one side convex where the other is concave, creating visual rhythm

### Prompt language
> "massively muscular legs with each muscle group clearly defined — teardrop-shaped quads, diamond calves, sweeping outer quad contrasted against the inner thigh curve"
>
> "powerful thighs with asymmetric inner and outer contours, each muscle group a distinct rounded mass, not drumstick-shaped"

## 8. Quick-Reference Cheat Sheet

Use this block as a mental checklist when writing any FMG character description in a panel prompt:

| Body Area | Key Rule | Anti-Pattern to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Overall figure | Hourglass, all curves, no angles | Reverse triangle, angular shapes |
| Head/hands/feet | Stay small and feminine | Enlarging proportionally with muscles |
| Biceps/triceps | Massive, always visible, distinct shapes | Blended blob, only visible when flexed |
| Forearms | Flowing contour, short wrist | Drumstick shape, long wrist |
| Breasts | Round spheres, gravity-defying, high | Teardrop, sagging, flat |
| Abs — shape | Square but rounded/pillowy, slight asymmetry, upright V-taper | Blocky, hard-edged, symmetric, inverted V |
| Abs — width | ~50% of breast width, not pinched narrow | Too narrow / compressed to centerline |
| Abs — shading | Top-lit (highlight on top, shadow under), matching breast lighting | Reversed shading (flabby look), inconsistent light direction |
| Abs — lower | Taper and recede into pelvis, smaller than upper abs | Abrupt termination, same size as upper abs |
| Abs — 3/4 view | Follow the same angle as the torso | Facing straight-on when body is turned |
| Obliques/waist | Thoracic portion (upper) more detailed, flank pad (lower) simpler | Detail priority inverted, or obliques omitted entirely |
| Lats | Hinted for balanced figure | Omitted (top-heavy look) |
| Butt | Equal to or wider than shoulders | Narrower than shoulders (masculine) |
| Butt + breasts | Proportionally balanced | One much larger than the other |
| Legs | Asymmetric inner/outer contours | Symmetric drumstick shape |
| Traps/shoulders | Small, rounded, not bulging | Large/boulder traps (reads male) |

## 9. Prompt Template Fragment

Drop this into any panel prompt where an FMG character is described at full muscle size:

```
[Character Name] has an hourglass figure with massively exaggerated muscles
but a small feminine head, delicate hands, and compact feet. Enormous round biceps
and thick triceps are clearly separated and visible even at rest. Forearms taper
with flowing muscular contours to short feminine wrists. Large perfectly round
breasts sit high on the chest, gravity-defying. Defined six-pack abs in an upright
V-taper — wider under the chest, narrowing toward the lower abs — each segment
smooth and rounded like bread buns, stretching to half the breast width. Top-lit
with highlights on the upper surface matching the breast lighting. Detailed upper
obliques (thoracic portion) with simpler lower flanks preserve the feminine waist
curve. Lower abs taper smoothly into the pelvis. Hinted lat spread gives a balanced
powerful frame. Large round glutes balance the breast size, hips and butt merging
into one flowing shape. Massive thighs with distinct quad sweep and diamond calves,
asymmetric inner and outer contours. All body contours use smooth flowing curves —
no angular or blocky shapes anywhere.
```

Adjust muscle size descriptors based on your 1–6 scale (reference `assets/muscle-size-references.json`). This fragment is for scale 5–6 (maximum). For lower scales, reduce the intensity of descriptors but keep the same anatomical rules.
